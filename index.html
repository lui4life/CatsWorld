Familiarize with Every Breeds

Maine Coon

Maine Coon is a large, long-haired domesticated cat breed originating in the U.S. state of Maine, where it is the official state cat.


The "Gentle Giant." These cats are known for their massive size, social personalities, and bushy tails that look like raccoons.

Siamese

Is one of the first distinctly recognised breeds of cat. It is selectively bred since the end of the 19th-century from the Wichianmat landrace


Elegant and talkative. Siamese cats have striking blue eyes and a unique "pointed" coat that changes color with temperature.

Bengal

The Bengal cat is a breed of hybrid cat created from crossbreeding an Asian leopard cat to domestic cats (Prionailurus bengalensis & Felis catus), especially the spotted tabby Egyptian Mau.


Athletic and wild-looking. These cats are descended from Asian Leopard Cats and love to climb, jump, and even swim!

Sphynx

is a breed of cat known for its lack of fur. Hairlessness in cats is a naturally occurring genetic mutation, and the Sphynx was developed through selective breeding of these animals


Unique and hairless. Though they look different, they are incredibly affectionate and love cuddling to stay warm.

Persian

The Persian cat, also known as the Persian Longhair or simply Persian, is a long-haired traditional breed of cat characterised by a round face and petite, but not flat and not smashed in, muzzle.


The two breeds were combined in order to produce a sturdy, short-nosed cat with a coat that was silky in texture, yet dense and full of body.
